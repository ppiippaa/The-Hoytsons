Our Story

We first met in 2013, when Natalie joined the geology graduate program at Iowa State University.

Although we were in the same department, we didn’t really interact at first. However, that changed one Saturday morning when we met up with several other graduate students to paint the student lounge. In the process, John hit Natalie in the head with a painting pole – there was a little blood and a lot of apologizing! – and our relationship developed from there. This may not be the most romantic beginning to a love story, but evidently the caveman approach worked for John!

Now, after more than 7 years and many adventures together, we are excited to start our next chapter as husband and wife. We can’t wait to celebrate our marriage with you!

Venue

River House & Dance Barn, 1314 Fig Avenue, Coon Rapids, IA 50058

Our ceremony and reception will be held at the historic River House & Dance Barn in Whiterock Conservancy. The venue is situated along the banks of the Middle Racoon River and surrounded by beautiful gardens and prairie. The barn itself was originally constructed in 1899, but was converted to a dance barn during Prohibition. It has been said that there has never been a “bad party” here!

Whiterock Conservancy

Whiterock Conservancy is a 5,500-acre non-profit land trust located near Coon Rapids, Iowa. The Conservancy is open to the public and offers visitors the chance to enjoy the rolling hills, landscape, and wildlife of the Middle Racoon River Valley. There are also many opportunities for recreation, with over 40 miles of trails for walkers, hikers, runners, mountain bikers, equestrians, and paddlers. In addition, Whiterock offers accommodations, camping, fishing, disc golf, and other outdoor activities.

Information for Overseas Visitors

Des Moines International Airport is the closest airport to our home in central Iowa, but you will likely find much cheaper tickets if you fly into Chicago O’Hare or Minneapolis. These airports are several hours away from us, but we will happily drive to pick you up. Alternatively, you have the option to book a connecting flight from Chicago O’Hare to Fort Dodge (a small regional airport ~50 miles from us), or take a Greyhound bus from either Chicago or Minneapolis.

Please note that you will require a tourist visa (ESTA) to enter the United States. The fee for this is $14 and you can apply online at https://esta.cbp.dhs.gov/esta (make sure that you apply directly through this link, and not through a third-party service).

As of March 22nd, the following Covid-19 restrictions are still in effect: Air passengers are required to be fully vaccinated and to provide proof of vaccination status prior to boarding a plane to the United States. Air passengers must show a negative Covid-19 test taken no more than 1 day before travel to the United States.

We are so excited for you to visit us in Iowa! Please reach out if you have any questions or need help with your travel plans.

Things To Do

Iowa

Iowa is a state in the American Midwest, bordered by the Mississippi River to the east and the Missouri River to the west. Our hometown of Boone is located in central Iowa, about 50 miles northwest of the state capitol Des Moines. Although Iowa may not feature on most travel bucket lists, it has plenty to offer, including beautiful scenery, great food, and a rapidly expanding wine scene. Here are a few of our favourite things to see/do:

  • Ledges State Park (Boone, IA)
  • Boone & Scenic Valley Railroad (Boone, IA)
  • The High Trestle Trail (Madrid, IA)
  • Alluvial Brewing Co. & Prairie Moon Winery (Ames, IA)
  • Living History Farms (Urbandale, IA)
  • Iowa Capitol Building (Des Moines, IA)
  • Des Moines Farmers Market (Des Moines, IA)
  • Adventureland Park (Des Moines, IA)
  • Summerset Winery (Indianola, IA)
  • Valle drive-in movie theater (Newton, IA)
  • Lake Red Rock
  • Pikes Peak State Park (McGregor, IA)
  • Great River Road National Scenic Byway
  • Loess Hills National Scenic Byway
Further Afield

Iowa is bordered by several other Midwest states, so if you’d like to explore a little further afield, the following cities are a (relatively) short drive away:

FAQs

  • What Time Should I Arrive?

  • Is There a Dress Code?
    Not at all. The bridal party will be in formal/semi-formal attire, and we encourage you to dress up too, but please feel free to wear whatever you are most comfortable in. Weather permitting, the ceremony and dinner will be held outdoors, so be sure to dress for the weather.
  • Can I bring a Date?
    Absolutely! If we failed to indicate this on your invitation, please know that we are happy for you to bring a plus one, whether that is a romantic partner or close friend. Please just let us know when you RSVP.
  • Will Food and Drinks Be Served at the Reception?

We will also provide plenty of beer, wine, and non-alcoholic beverages. If there is a particular drink that you would like to bring for yourself, or to share, you are welcome to do so, but please be aware that our venue does not allow hard liquor.
